A common platform for change

A Personal Project

Jess Lee

Politically aware Millennial

Jess is a 19 year old US citizen studying architecture at Parsons university. She is concious of global events and is particularly passionate about the environment. Coming from Hong kong, she knows the impacts of pollution and bad waste managment.

Experience Goals

Jess would like to channel her voice and thoughts into impactful movements and campaigns. She would like an easy experience to connect her with local and international campaigns making her voice heard.

Task Goals

Jess Lee would like a platform where she is able to visualise all the campaigns running in her local community. Jess would like to record her interest in an app and get targeted information inviting her to an event. She would like live updates to be transmitted to her phone during an event to keep her updated on any special messages or changes to plan/event location.

Pain Points

Difficulty of being informed of what campaigns are going on in the community.

Getting updated on the status of an event is complicated and confusing as different events use different platforms/social media sites.

Wireframing

Homepage

On this page you see all the protests you have signed up for. And a list of your likes and views in #tag form.

Discover Page

On this page you will see all the running and upcoming protests. These may be viewed in two options. “Nearby” and “popular”.

Going Page

This page shows you an overview of all the protests you have signed up for. Notification badges indicate any important messages or uptates. The dates are clearly indicated next to the protest name. These areordered in chronological order.

Protest Card

Protest cards give an overview of the protest as it is. It provides information such as sponsors, timing, location etc. This helps you decide if you wish to follow it or not.

Update Page

This page shows the protester any necessary updates to look out for. These updates are pushed to your phone and may consist of anything the organiser wishes to share. The most recent update is highlighted in red and past updates in grey.

Map Page

On this page a detailed view of the organised march is presented. The start and end location are clearly indicated. All information provided by AppleMaps. The map may also be exported to its native app.

Checklist Page

On this page, a checklist is presented. This checklist has been personally curated by the organisers. This helps remind protesters what to bring. (gives an interaction for the protesters)

Supporters Page

On this page you will see the logos of the 10 largest supporters for your protest. This can be in money form or simply support. Individuals may also donate to the page to help the cause (Through ApplePay)
