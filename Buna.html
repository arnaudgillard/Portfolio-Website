Re-developing the coffee experience

Group work, 11 weeks

Jean-Francois

Appreciation for great coffee

Jean-Francois is a recently retired (52 year old) French father now living in Cannes. He drinks coffee on a regular basis whilst he reads in his summer house. Jean-Francois has an eye for interior design and searches for unique products to add to his collection.

Experience Goals

Jean-Francois wants a coffee maker which enhances his experience with the drink. He would like the artisanal/manual experience of making coffee with an element of technological automation to assist him.

Task Goals

Jean-Francois wants to make the perfect cup of coffee in his summer house. He would like the coffee maker to serve several people. Interaction with the product is imperative to give an authentic artisanal feeling.

Pain Points

Coffee Makers are dull and lack personal interaction.

Buna

BRINGING ARTISANAL COFFEE INTO THE HOME

IDEATION

GROUP SKETCHES

With the history researched and the technology proven, the group set out to ideate many different iterations of form and function. The team was strong with vivid brainstorming and creative thinking which resulted in a unique design ideal for the summer house environment.

MODEL DEVELOPMENT

BLUE FOAM & SCHEMATIC MODEL

Once we narrowed down our designs we set out to develop them as physical prototypes. User testing was organised to help improve the user flow and experience. Different materials and colours were used to differentiate key design elements and considerations.

A Visual and Schematic model were produced to test both the technology and user experience. The schematic model predominantly included components from a disassembled coffee machine which was provided at the start of the project. Engineering drawings were then later produced to guarantee component fit. It was also an opportunity to identify key elements of our design for manufacture.

STORYBOARDING

VISUALISING THE USER EXPERIENCE

The storyboard emphasises the intricate yet intuitive steps needed to create the best cup of coffee. The coffee is brewed using a siphoning technique containing two chambers of vapour pressure and vacuumes. This traditional method was prized for it’s clear brew and is being brought back with Buna.
